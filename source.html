About Me

Karina Khaledi is a second-year at Northeastern University pursuing a degree in Computer Science and Business Administration with a concentration in Finance.

Karina developed a passion for computer science when her high school did not offer many coding courses, thus she took matters into her own hands and registered for Python, Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence, Java, and HTML-CSS-Javascript courses at a collegiate level at Columbia University before entering college. However, since then, Karina has broadened her portfolio into the finance and venture capital world. Karina has a desire to be at the intersection of Computer Science and Business in order to delve into both of her dreams.

Karina’s website represents access to showcase her experiance as a coder and business student. She has accomplished many projects ranging from experiance in a Fintech start-up, creating a non-profit organization to help bridge the gender and geographical barriers for women interested in coding, and even to being a teacher’s assistant at Columbia University’s Business School Venture For All program.
